# Code of conduct

Be kind. Assume the person on the other end is smart, busy, and working with
less context than you have.

That covers most of it, but specifics help:

**Critique code, not people.** "This drops the response when two clients race"
is useful. "This is sloppy" is not, and it is not more true for being blunt.

**Explain the why.** If you are asking for a change, say what goes wrong without
it. The other person should be able to disagree with you intelligently.

**No condescension.** Skip "simply", "just", "obviously". Something being easy
for you says nothing about whether it is easy, and those words land on whoever
found it hard.

**Nobody owes you a response.** This is a side project. Maintainers have
families and jobs. A ping after a week is fine; a second one the same day is
not.

**Off limits:** harassment, personal attacks, sexual attention, publishing
someone's private information, and sustained disruption of a thread.

## Scope and enforcement

This applies in issues, pull requests, commits and any other project space.

Report problems to <mario@djanic.com>. I read every report and keep it private.
Depending on what happened, the response is a private word, a public correction,
or a ban. I would rather have a small project than a hostile one.

I am the only maintainer, so I am also the appeals process. That is not ideal
and I know it. If a decision looks wrong to you, say so and I will reconsider.
